Preflight · gauge–Higgs orbifold models

Is your Wilson-line fundamental domain legal?

Most computations of a one-loop Wilson-line potential on T²/ℤ₂ halve the search region in α₂. That halving rests on a hypothesis about your representation, and for every representation able to host a Standard-Model quark generation the hypothesis fails. Nothing warns you: a restricted minimiser returns a boundary point that looks like a vacuum. This check costs one parity bit.

Describe your model

Highest-weight labels of an irreducible representation of SU(4).

One row per Kaluza–Klein tower: m, q, multiplicity. Both pairings are measured here, not inferred, so this path stays valid for spectra outside SU(4).

One row per diagonal slot of the group element the orbifold hands you: twist sign (±1), Wilson-line charge. The rule exists only when a unit shift equals a central element up to a Weyl reflection.

Method. Advancing the Wilson line by one period is, up to a Weyl reflection, multiplication by the central element −1 of Z(SU(4)). A representation answers a central element with the scalar ζc(R) — that is what n-ality is — so the character obeys D(−t) = (−1)a+2b+3c D(t). Twist multiplicities therefore pair at even m when the centre charge is odd and at odd m when it is even, never both, outside a degenerate class classified exactly and certified in Lean 4.
